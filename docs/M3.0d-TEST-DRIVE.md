> Date drafted: 2026-06-09

This document is the script for the **first user-facing M3 deliverable** — SG Dietz personally voice-driving 6 through a contractor search end-to-end on mock data. The goal is to validate that the M3.0 foundation (overlay UI + transcript wiring + intent classifier + context injection) actually unblocks the voice-first vision before we pour weeks into M3.1 phone calling and beyond.

If this works, M3.1+ is worth building. If 6's narration feels disconnected from the drawer, or the rhythm is off, we tune before adding surface area.

---

## TL;DR for SG Dietz

You'll open the site, click Start, and talk to 6 like you would to a person who's helping you find a plumber. **Don't type. Don't click anything in the drawer.** Just talk. The cards that appear on the right are 6's notes; the words you hear are 6's voice. If both feel like they belong together, we've proven the architecture.

The whole flow is 4 utterances and takes about 90 seconds:

1. *"Find me a plumber near Austin."*
2. *"Tell me more about the first one."*
3. *"Which one should I pick?"*
4. *"Book that one."*

You're allowed (encouraged) to deviate. Phrase things how you naturally would. If 6 doesn't understand, say it differently. The point is to see how it feels.

## The Test Drive — Step by Step

### Step 0 — Open the page and start

1. Open **`http://localhost:3001/en`** in the browser of your choice (Chrome / Safari / Firefox — all fine).
2. Open **DevTools** (Cmd-Opt-I / F12) → **Network tab** → filter by `transcripts` so you can watch the orchestrator firing as you speak.
3. Click **Start** on the avatar UI. Wait for 6 to greet you.

### Step 1 — Find a plumber

**Say:** *"Hey 6, find me a plumber near Austin."*

**What should happen, in order:**

| Time | What you see / hear |
|---|---|
| ~0.5s after you stop talking | 6 begins responding (something like *"Sure, let me check…"* — HeyGen brain's initial reply) |
| ~1.0s | DevTools Network tab fires a `POST /api/transcripts/append`. Click it → Response panel should show `orchestrator.kind: "action"`, `classification.kind: "find_contractor"`, `classification.confidence: "high"` |
| ~1.0s | **Right-side drawer slides in** with 5–10 ranked plumber cards. Each card: name, ★ rating, distance km, $/$$/$$$, badges (Licensed / Same day / Locally owned), phone/website links |
| ~1.5s after 6's initial reply | 6 speaks again — this time naming the top plumber: *"I found 5 plumbers nearby. The top one is Acme Plumbing — 4.8 stars and just 2 km from you. Want me to tell you more, or should I go with them?"* |

**What to look for:**
- ✅ Does the cards drawer animate in cleanly and not flicker?
- ✅ Does 6 mention the actual top contractor's name (which matches what's in card #1)?
- ✅ Does the rhythm feel like "looking something up" rather than "two voices talking over each other"?
- ❌ If 6 says generic stuff like *"I'm searching now"* without ever naming a contractor → the context injection failed. Check Network response for `orchestrator.contextMessage`; if it's there but 6 didn't say it, the issue is in HeyGen Persona tuning (HeyGen is ignoring the wrapped context).
- ❌ If the drawer doesn't appear → check `orchestrator.variant` in Network response. If `variant.kind === "contractors"` is present, the Zustand store wiring is broken.

### Step 2 — Drill into the top pick

**Say:** *"Tell me more about the first one."* (or *"What are people saying about Acme?"* — both should work)

**What should happen:**

| Time | What you see / hear |
|---|---|
| ~0.5s | 6 begins a transition: *"Let me pull up the reviews…"* |
| ~1.0s | Network fires append + the orchestrator returns. Response: `classification.kind: "tell_me_more"`, `slots.contractor_ref: { type: "ordinal", position: 1 }` (or `name`-based if you said "Acme") |
| ~1.5s | **Drawer swaps** from contractors list to the summary panel — overview sentence at top, green Strengths bullets, rose Watch-outs bullets, 2–3 italic sample quotes with star ratings, *"Fresh"* badge top-right (or *"Cached"* if already generated previously) |
| ~3–5s for first time, ~1s if cached | 6 narrates: *"They get a lot of praise for showing up on time and clean pricing. The one watch-out is occasional scheduling delays during busy weeks. Want to go with them?"* |

Note the *first-time* summary generation takes 3–5 seconds (gpt-4o-mini call). Cached is instant.

**What to look for:**
- ✅ Does 6 paraphrase the strengths/watch-outs in their own words rather than reading the bullets verbatim?
- ✅ Do the sample quotes in the drawer feel real (not generic boilerplate)?
- ❌ If 6 says *"I don't have any details about them"* → check `orchestrator.contextMessage`; if it starts with `[INTENT NOT ACTIONABLE]`, the contractor reference resolution failed. Either your snapshot wasn't sent (Network tab body shows no `surface_snapshot`) or the contractor list moved between turns.

### Step 3 — Ask for a recommendation

**Say:** *"Which one should I pick?"* (or *"I can't decide"*, *"What do you recommend?"* — all work)

**What should happen:**

| Time | What you see / hear |
|---|---|
| ~0.5s | 6 begins: *"OK, let me think about which fits you best…"* |
| ~1.5–3s | Network fires. Response: `classification.kind: "recommend"`. **Drawer swaps** to gold-bordered "6's top picks" panel — 3 ranked picks numbered #1/#2/#3, each with name + ★/km/$ + a 1-line reason. If you've talked to 6 before about preferences, top-of-panel shows *"Tuned by what 6 remembers: ..."* |
| ~3–6s | 6 narrates the #1 pick + reason: *"I'd go with Acme — they're top-rated, the closest to you, and they're locally-owned which I remember you prefer."* |

**What to look for:**
- ✅ Does the reason 6 says match what's in the #1 card on screen?
- ✅ If you're signed in and have memory facts, does the panel surface them with *"Tuned by what 6 remembers:"*?

### Step 4 — Confirm the booking

**Say:** *"Book that one."* (or *"Let's go with Acme"*, *"I'll take the first one"*)

**What should happen:**

| Time | What you see / hear |
|---|---|
| ~0.5s | 6 begins: *"On it…"* |
| ~1s | Network fires. Response: `classification.kind: "book"`. **Drawer swaps** to emerald "6 has notified the candidates" panel — winner card (with channel + delivered status), list of other candidates with their channel + status (mostly "failed" — see note below) |
| ~1.5s | 6 confirms: *"Done — Acme Plumbing will be in touch shortly. I also let the other candidates know with some friendly feedback for next time."* |

**v1 caveat:** In this test drive the book intent is **mocked** — it doesn't actually fire real M2.6 notifications (that requires sign-in and real vendor config). The drawer shows synthetic delivery statuses. When this checkpoint passes and the test drive ships to signed-in users, the mock swaps for a real fan-out — that's a 5-line change in `orchestrator.ts`.

## Exit Criteria

The test drive is **accepted** when SG Dietz can sign off that:

- [ ] All 4 utterances above produced a meaningful spoken response from 6 AND a corresponding drawer state
- [ ] 6's narration referenced actual data (contractor names, ratings, reasons) — not generic placeholder responses
- [ ] The rhythm felt like "6 is looking something up and reporting back" — not "two voices talking over each other"
- [ ] No technical glitches (audio dropout, drawer flicker, missing data) that broke immersion

It is **NOT** accepted (and we tune before M3.1) if:

- 6 never mentions specific contractor data even when the orchestrator response shows `contextMessage` was sent
- The drawer fails to populate after a clear `variant` was returned
- HeyGen's brain talks over itself when context injection arrives
- The same intent works on one phrasing but not a slight rephrasing that any human would understand

## Troubleshooting

### "I said something and nothing happened"

1. Check DevTools Network for `transcripts/append` — did it fire?
2. If yes, check response. `orchestrator.kind === "none"` means the classifier didn't match. Try rephrasing — the v1 classifier is rules-based and tolerates limited phrasing.
3. If `orchestrator.kind === "action"` but `variant` is missing, the intent matched but `confidence === "medium"` (missing slots). Check `debug` field for the missing slots.

### "6 talks but the drawer is empty / wrong"

1. Open DevTools Console. Check for *"transcripts append failed"* or *"session.message threw"* warnings.
2. Check Network → response → `orchestrator.variant`. If `kind: "contractors"` has `hits: []`, the search returned nothing — check that you seeded contractors AND that the location matches (default is Austin if no city detected).

### "The drawer appears but 6 still says generic stuff"

This is a **HeyGen Persona tuning issue**, not a code bug. The Persona config in HeyGen Dashboard must instruct the brain to **read context messages tagged `[XYZ — not spoken by user]` literally as data, narrate them in first person, and never speak the wrapper tag aloud.** See the next section.

### "I said 'tell me more about Acme' but 6 says it doesn't know who Acme is"

The contractor-by-name resolver is a Supabase ILIKE on `contractors.name`. If the seeded contractors don't include an "Acme" prefix, the resolver returns null. Workaround: use the ordinal form (*"the first one"*).

### Stuck transcripts (no append firing)

The `useTranscriptCapture` hook subscribes once when the LiveAvatar session connects. If the session has been running for a long time and stops firing transcription events, try **Stop + Start** to re-establish the session.

## HeyGen Persona / Knowledge Base Config

**Where this goes:** HeyGen Dashboard → Avatars → (your avatar) → Persona / Knowledge Base panel. Paste the entire text below into the appropriate field (usually a single multi-line textbox). Save. Restart any open avatar sessions to pick up the new config.

**Why this is needed:** without this, HeyGen's FULL-mode brain doesn't know that messages tagged `[CONTEXT — not spoken by user]` are data fed by our backend. It might read the wrapper tag aloud, paraphrase the data poorly, or ignore the instructions inside. The context-injection pattern depends on the brain treating wrapped messages exactly as intended.

```
You are 6, the AI digital twin of SG Dietz at iSolveUrProblems.ai.
You help homeowners diagnose problems and find vetted contractors.
Speak in first person as 6. Be warm, encouraging, technically clear
when needed, and never sarcastic. Match the user's tone and language.

──── BACKEND CONTEXT INJECTION (CRITICAL) ────

You will frequently receive messages that start with a bracketed tag
like `[CONTRACTOR SEARCH — not spoken by user]` or `[REVIEW SUMMARY
— not spoken by user]`. These are NOT user messages. They are facts
the iSolveUrProblems platform's backend has just computed for you.

When you receive such a message:
  1. NEVER read the wrapper tag aloud. The user cannot see it.
  2. NEVER read the data inside it verbatim like a list. Synthesize.
  3. Use the data inside as ground truth — these are real contractors,
     real ratings, real distances, real reasons. Don't invent names,
     numbers, or details that aren't there.
  4. Follow the instructions at the end of the wrapper (usually a
     directive like "respond in two sentences, name the top pick,
     mention rating and distance, offer to tell more or pick one").
  5. The user is also seeing visual cards for this data on a side
     panel. You don't need to list everything — you can say
     "here are some good options" and let them see the cards.

──── WHILE WAITING ────

When a user asks you to find something or pick something, your
first response (before the backend has returned) should be brief
and conversational, like:
  - "Sure, let me check."
  - "Give me a second to look those up."
  - "Let me think about which fits you best."

Never make up specific contractor names, ratings, or distances in
this "while waiting" response. Wait for the real data to arrive in
a context-message and narrate it then.

──── HOMEOWNER-FACING TONE ────

This is a real homeowner who may have a real problem. Be respectful
of their time and money. Don't oversell. Acknowledge constraints
honestly (e.g. if budget is mentioned, prioritize lower price tier;
if locally-owned is mentioned, recommend accordingly).

When you book a contractor for a homeowner, confirm with their name
and acknowledge that other candidates were notified with friendly
feedback. Don't be overly enthusiastic — be confident and warm.

──── LANGUAGE ────

Respond in the user's language. The platform supports English,
Spanish, French, Portuguese, German, and Chinese. Match the
language the user is speaking; switch if they switch.
```
